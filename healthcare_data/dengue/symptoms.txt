UNDERSTANDING DENGUE
OVERVIEW
Dengue is a mosquito-borne viral disease caused by the dengue virus. The disease is transmitted primarily through the bite of infected Aedes mosquitoes, especially Aedes aegypti. Dengue is common in tropical and subtropical regions and remains a major public health concern in many countries.
Dengue infections may range from mild illness to severe disease. Early recognition of symptoms and proper medical evaluation are important for preventing complications.
CAUSES AND TRANSMISSION
Dengue is caused by infection with the dengue virus. The virus is transmitted when an infected mosquito bites a person. Mosquitoes become infected after feeding on a person who already carries the virus.
Dengue is not usually spread directly from one person to another through casual contact, touching, sharing food, or being near an infected individual.
Mosquitoes that transmit dengue often breed in clean standing water found in containers, buckets, flower pots, tires, and water storage containers.
WHO IS AT RISK
Anyone can become infected with dengue. However, some people may have a higher risk of exposure, including:
People living in areas where dengue is common
Individuals living near mosquito breeding sites
Travelers visiting regions with dengue outbreaks
Children and older adults
People with previous dengue infections
COMMON SYMPTOMS
Dengue symptoms can vary from person to person. Common symptoms include:
High fever
Severe headache
Pain behind the eyes
Muscle pain
Joint pain
Nausea
Vomiting
Skin rash
Fatigue
Weakness
Loss of appetite
Many patients describe dengue as causing intense body aches and extreme tiredness.
EARLY SYMPTOMS
Early symptoms often include:
Sudden fever
Headache
General body discomfort
Muscle aches
Fatigue
Symptoms may initially resemble other viral illnesses, making medical evaluation important.
WHEN SYMPTOMS APPEAR
Symptoms typically appear several days after infection. The illness usually develops after a mosquito bite from an infected mosquito.
DURATION OF ILLNESS
Most dengue infections improve within approximately one week. Some individuals may continue experiencing fatigue and weakness even after the fever has resolved.
MILD AND SEVERE CASES
Many people recover without serious complications. However, some infections can progress to severe dengue, which requires immediate medical attention.
FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
What is dengue?
Dengue is a viral disease transmitted through infected mosquito bites.
How is dengue spread?
Dengue spreads through mosquitoes carrying the dengue virus.
Is dengue contagious?
Dengue is generally not spread directly from one person to another.
Can children get dengue?
Yes. Dengue can affect children, adolescents, adults, and older individuals.
Can older adults get dengue?
Yes. Older adults may experience more severe complications.
Can a person get dengue more than once?
Yes. Multiple dengue infections may occur.
Does everyone experience the same symptoms?
No. Symptoms and severity may differ among individuals.
Can dengue be mild?
Yes. Some infections are mild while others may become severe.
Can dengue cause a rash?
Yes. A skin rash may develop during illness.
Can dengue cause body pain?
Yes. Muscle pain and joint pain are common symptoms.
Can dengue cause fatigue?
Yes. Fatigue and weakness are frequently reported.
USER CONCERNS
I have a fever and body pain.
Fever and body pain may occur in dengue and several other illnesses. Medical evaluation is recommended when symptoms persist or worsen.
I feel weak and exhausted.
Fatigue and weakness are commonly reported by dengue patients.
I have a headache and pain behind my eyes.
Headaches and eye pain are recognized symptoms associated with dengue.
I developed a rash while having a fever.
Rash may occur in dengue infection and should be monitored together with other symptoms.
I am worried that I may have dengue.
Only a qualified healthcare professional can diagnose dengue. Medical assessment may be necessary when symptoms are concerning.
